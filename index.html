About Me

I'm a Researcher at the Ideker Lab, UCSD, where I work on AI co-scientist agents for biological research. My current focus is the development and evaluation of AI agents with the goal to help wet-lab scientists accellerate their daily work, just as coding agents help software developers.

I am the former Director of Software development and director of the Cytoscape project at the Ideker Lab, but with the emergence of powerful LLMs, I shifted to hands-on research on how to use them for science. As part of that shift, I'm also pursuing a late-career doctorate in the Bioinformatics and Systems Biology program at UCSD.

I retained my role as Principle Investigator for the NDEx Project (ndexbio.org), "The Network Data Exchange" - a Network Commons that serves as the backbone for the Cytoscape ecosystem, enabling scientists worldwide to store, share, and publish biological networks.
